# India CPI Inflation Analysis (2016–2023)

## Overview

This project analyzes India's Consumer Price Index (CPI) data released by the National Statistical Office (NSO) to understand inflation trends, category-level contributions, food inflation dynamics, COVID-19 impacts, and the influence of global crude oil prices on inflation.

Using Python and exploratory data analysis techniques, the project investigates the key drivers of inflation and identifies categories that contributed most significantly to price increases between 2016 and 2023.

---

## Tools & Technologies

Microsoft Excel: Data cleaning, analysis, visualization, Statistical Functions: =CORREL, =AVERAGE, etc, 
Charts Used: Line Graphs, Combo Charts, Correlation Tables.

---

## Dataset

The dataset contains monthly CPI index values published by the National Statistical Office (NSO), Government of India.


---


## Key Findings

• Analyzed 2013-2023 Government of India CPI Inflation data using Excel, applying Data Cleaning & Statistical Analysis
techniques to evaluate category-wise CPI distribution, identifying Food as the largest contributor, accounting for approx
imately 48% of the inflation basket.
• Performed Time-Series Analysis of CPI General Index (2017-2023) and identified the 2022 inflation peak of 6.62%, driven
by post-pandemic supply-chain constraints, elevated food prices & global crude oil fluctuations.
• Investigated MoM food inflation trends(Jun’22- May’23) , identifying the highest spike of 1.04% in June 2022, with Spices
recording nearly 17% YoY inflation.
• Performed Correlation Analysis between crude oil fluctuations annd CPI categories, revealing strong positive correlation in
Oils & Fats(0.81), Meat & Fish (0.77), and Transport (0.68).
